8 CONCLUSION¶

In this project, I chose a decision tree regression model of degree 2 to predict the target variable based on a set of features. The diabetes dataset was split into training and testing sets, and the model was trained using the training data. Subsequently, its performance was evaluated on the testing data.¶

Performance Summary:¶

The decision tree regression model achieved relative satisfactory performance on the testing data, with R^2 of 0.22.¶

Model Limitations:¶

Despite its performance, the decision tree regression model has certain limitations:¶

Overfitting: Decision trees are prone to overfitting, especially when they are deep or have many branches. This can lead to poor generalization performance on unseen data.¶

Model Failures:¶

While the decision tree regression model performed well overall, it failed in certain scenarios aspects:¶

Nonlinear Relationships: If the relationship between the features and the target variable is highly nonlinear as seen in the graph, and cannot be captured by the decision tree's splits, the model may fail to accurately predict the target variable.¶
